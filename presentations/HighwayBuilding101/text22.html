Persistent Identifier (PID)

  • Why is the PID important?

    • An essential technology to preserve “referential integrity”.
    • Approximately 41% or the URLs referenced in Computer and CACM journals in the period 1995-1999 were inaccessible in 2002 (Spinellis, 2003)

  • What is it?

    • An identifier that is technology and protocol independent and is mapped to a URL.
    • Example: The historic newspaper “The Poultryman”
    • The handle for an issue is 1782.1/poultryman.6.39
    • URL access: http://hdl.rutgers.edu/1782.1/poultryman.6.39

  • CNRI Handle System (http://www.handle.net)

    • For assigning, managing and resolving persistent identifiers
    • Managed by the Corporation for National Research Initiatives
